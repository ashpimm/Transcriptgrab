# Promote.dev Product

Last updated: 23 July 2026

## One sentence

Promote.dev turns one product link into complete, faceless social posts built from hook patterns already outperforming in the product's buyers' niche, with downloads, Reel exports, and daily Instagram publishing.

## Platform

Web application.

## Primary customer

Solo app founders, indie hackers, and small SaaS teams who can build a useful product but do not have a repeatable way to market it through short-form social content.

Their typical context:

- The product is shipped or close to launch.
- They know consistent social visibility matters but do not know what to post.
- Researching trends, writing, designing, filming, and editing feel like a second profession.
- They are camera-shy, time-poor, or both.
- They want a finished asset, not another AI draft that creates more homework.
- They care more about reaching their buyers than performing for other founders.

Secondary customers include lean product-marketing teams and small agencies promoting app or SaaS clients. They are not addressed in the primary hero because founder specificity converts more clearly.

## Job to be done

When a founder has a product worth promoting but no reliable content workflow, help them go from a product link and a blank page to a complete post that can be downloaded immediately or published automatically—without researching trends, writing copy, designing slides, filming, or editing.

## Customer problem

Builders can ship software, but distribution requires a separate set of skills:

- Knowing which topics and openings earn attention
- Understanding what the product's buyers consume
- Turning product features into useful, non-advertorial content
- Creating a coherent hook-to-payoff story
- Designing every asset
- Publishing often enough to learn what resonates

Generic AI tools remove typing. Promote.dev removes the workflow.

## Core value proposition

> Turn your app into posts people stop for.

Promote.dev combines evidence-backed creative research with buyer-niche adaptation and finished production.

Most AI content tools begin with a prompt and produce plausible ideas. Promote.dev begins with recent spoken openings that measurably outperformed their source accounts, determines what the product's actual buyers watch, transfers the winning structure to the product, and produces the complete post.

The unfair advantage is the combination:

1. **Evidence:** every public source carries inspectable view, follower, and outlier receipts.
2. **Buyer relevance:** content is generated in the customer's niche, not automatically in the founder or app-development niche.
3. **Finished output:** six designed slides, caption, hashtags, CTA, downloads, and optional Reel.
4. **Distribution:** Pro can prepare, queue, and publish a fresh Instagram post every day.

## What “proven” means

`Proven` applies to the source opening's ability to earn disproportionate attention. It does not mean the generated adaptation is guaranteed to achieve views, installs, revenue, or any other marketing outcome.

A public mined source must meet all current quality gates:

- Published within approximately 120 days
- At least 10,000 views
- At least 50 followers on the source account
- Views equal to at least 5x the account's followers
- A usable spoken transcript
- Relevant to the audience niche
- Suitable for the current English-language feed

The product preserves the source opening's structural mechanism—such as tension, contrast, specificity, stakes, or curiosity—while leaving the original creator's subject behind.

## Core product flow

### 1. Product handoff

The user pastes an App Store, Play Store, SaaS, or product-page URL. Promote.dev extracts a draft profile:

- Product name
- What the product helps people do
- Who needs it most
- Clearest customer outcome
- Verified product facts
- Brand color
- Buyer's content niche

The user reviews this profile once. It powers future posts.

### 2. Evidence-backed hook selection

Promote.dev searches the product's buyer niche for recent qualifying hooks. It uses AI to shortlist hooks whose winning mechanism can genuinely transfer to this product.

The user may choose a hook and visual style manually or let Promote.dev select both.

### 3. Complete post generation

Each generation creates one six-slide story:

- Slide 1: transplanted hook and clear promise
- Middle slides: useful, specific payoff that fulfills the promise
- Final slide: natural product conclusion and CTA
- Ready-to-post caption
- Up to eight niche-relevant hashtags
- Brand-colored design
- Photographic cover when appropriate

The content mix rotates:

- Approximately 75% genuinely useful buyer-niche content
- Approximately 25% direct product showcase

Product claims may come only from the saved product profile. The system may use established domain knowledge for educational slides but must not invent product features, customers, results, or statistics.

### 4. Export and publishing

Every completed post can be downloaded as lossless slide files with its caption.

Pro members can:

- Turn a finished post into a silent 1080x1920 Reel with readable timing and gentle movement
- Connect an eligible Instagram account
- Maintain a rolling daily publishing queue
- See scheduled posts and delivery status
- Recover safely from temporary provider failures without duplicate posting

Promote.dev speaks to people who ship products, not marketers who talk about marketing. It should feel confident without sounding like a growth-hacking course or an AI hype page. Confidence comes from visible proof and a finished deliverable.

## Copy principles

1. **Lead with the finished outcome.** Say `post` before `carousel generation`.
2. **Translate features into removed work.** Research, writing, design, filming, editing, and routine publishing are the jobs being eliminated.
3. **Use receipts instead of hype.** Explain the 5x rule and source quality floors.
4. **Separate evidence from guarantees.** Source hooks outperformed; future results are not promised.
5. **Make CTAs describe the result.** Prefer `Create my first free post` over `Start free`.
6. **Keep the audience narrow.** Speak to app and SaaS founders first.
7. **Name the buyer-niche advantage.** A product should receive content for its customers, not content for other builders.
8. **Use plain words.** Avoid revolutionary, next-gen, skyrocket, game-changer, unlock, elevate, and similar AI/SaaS filler.

## Claims policy

Safe claims:

- Source hooks earned at least 5x as many views as the creator had followers.
- Public mined hooks meet the documented freshness, view, follower, transcript, and relevance gates.
- Each post includes six slides, caption, hashtags, and CTA.
- The first three posts are free.
- Pro includes 30 posts per month, Reel exports, and daily Instagram publishing.
- No filming is required.

Do not claim:

- Guaranteed virality, views, followers, installs, leads, revenue, or ROI
- That a generated post itself is already proven
- That every post will use a photograph
- That publishing can bypass Instagram's account eligibility requirements
- Unsupported competitor pricing or savings
- User counts, customer results, or testimonials that have not been verified

## Visual system

The shipped visual system is pure black with white type and one highlighter-yellow signal (`#FFDD00`) reserved for evidence, generation, and primary action.

- The yellow behaves like a highlighter over the part that matters.
- Errors and destructive states use the separate danger red.
- User-generated slides use the customer's brand color, never Promote.dev yellow.
- Generated output should feel editorial and publishable, not like a branded product screenshot.
- The interface should stay export-first and avoid scheduler-dashboard clutter.

## Design principles

1. **Receipts, not claims.** Evidence should be the loudest supporting message.
2. **The free output is the pitch.** Let users experience a complete post before asking them to pay.
3. **Speed to first publishable post.** Remove anything that delays the finished result.
4. **Done for you by default.** Manual hook and style choices remain available but secondary.
5. **Export-first, automation when wanted.** Users keep control of every post even when daily publishing is enabled.
6. **Founder-native energy.** Bold and specific beats corporate and vague.

## Accessibility

Target WCAG 2.1 AA:

- Body text contrast of at least 4.5:1
- Large or bold text contrast of at least 3:1
- Keyboard-accessible controls with visible focus
- Reduced-motion alternatives
- No meaning communicated by color alone
- Readable slide typography remains the practical priority

## Current non-goals

- AI avatars or talking-head UGC generation
- Paid ad creation and campaign management
- Social-performance analytics ingestion
- Guaranteed growth outcomes
- Full multi-brand agency workspaces
- Enterprise approval workflows

These may become future products, but they should not dilute the current promise: one product link to complete social posts and daily Instagram visibility.
