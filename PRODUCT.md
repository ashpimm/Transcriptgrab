# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Short-form content creators, app founders, product owners, and small agencies who need viral hooks fast. Their context: they open Hooklab when facing a blank page — no idea what to post — and want proven, high-performing hook patterns from their buyer's niche that they can adapt to their product in minutes, not hours. They are creator-native, phone-first in spirit, allergic to "marketing tool" bloat. The job to be done: go from blank → a scripted, ready-to-shoot content pack backed by evidence that the hook already worked.

## Product Purpose

Hooklab mines viral outlier hooks (the 5x rule: views ≥ 5× account followers), templatizes them, and turns them into script packs in the user's voice plus AI faceless carousels. The hook library is the free front door; generation is Pro ($39/mo). Success looks like: a user lands, immediately sees hooks that obviously outperformed, saves a few, and feels the pull to generate their own pack — because the proof is right there on the card.

## Brand Personality

Bold, punchy, creator-native. Speaks the language of people who actually post, not marketers who talk about posting. Confident and high-energy without tipping into hype or hustle-guru theatrics — the swagger is *earned* because every hook ships with receipts (view counts, follower counts, outlier scores). Voice: direct, a little irreverent, zero corporate hedging. Emotional goal: the user feels equipped and slightly unfair-advantaged, like they got the cheat sheet.

Note: the current shipped look reads restrained/lab-precise (paper + ink + a single signal-orange). The owner wants it to feel bolder and more creator-native than it currently does — the personality above is the target direction, and the palette/energy is expected to move toward it. This is a redesign brief, not a description of the status quo.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS-cream landing** — warm-neutral background, gradient accent, hero-metric template. The 2026 AI default. Avoid.
- **Hypey "guru" course page** — neon gradients, countdown timers, fake urgency, testimonial-wall clutter, screaming CTAs. The confidence must come from proof, never from pressure.
- **Enterprise dashboard grey** — dense grey admin panels, flat Bootstrap cards, corporate blue, zero personality.
- **Cluttered social scheduler** — Buffer/Hootsuite-style crowded multi-panel tool UI with too many nav layers. Hooklab is export-first and gets out of the way.

## Design Principles

1. **Receipts, not claims.** Every hook carries proof it worked (views, followers, outlier score). The design's job is to make evidence the loudest thing on the card. Trust is earned on screen, never asserted in copy.
2. **The free library is the pitch.** Acquisition happens by letting people feel the value before paying. The upgrade should feel inevitable, not nagged — pull, not push.
3. **Creator-native energy, not marketer polish.** Talk and look like the people who post daily. Bold and direct beats safe and corporate. Restraint that reads as timid is a failure here.
4. **Export-first, get out of the way.** The tool exists to hand over a ready-to-use pack. No panel bloat, no scheduling maze — shortest path from blank page to shootable content.
5. **Speed to first hook.** Every screen is measured by how fast it delivers a usable hook or script. Cut anything that delays that moment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, large/bold text ≥ 3:1; keyboard-navigable with visible focus (signal-color focus ring already present); reduced-motion alternative for every animation (partly implemented via `prefers-reduced-motion`). Do not encode meaning in the accent color alone — pair the outlier/score signal with text or shape so it survives color-blindness. The owner is not tracking a formal standard; AA is adopted here as the sensible floor, and readability of hook text on cards is the practical priority.
