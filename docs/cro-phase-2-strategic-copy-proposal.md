# Phase 2: Strategic Copy Audit & Proposal

Date: 2026-07-22

Status: Approved with changes and implemented in Phase 3.

Decision record: the user selected **Promote.dev** instead of the proposed ShipSeen name. Founder-first positioning and `post` as the primary deliverable term were approved. Instagram auto-posting is fully operational, so the private-beta recommendation in this original proposal was superseded; implementation copy presents daily Instagram publishing as an available Pro feature.

## Positioning recommendation

### Category

Evidence-backed social content for apps and SaaS products.

### Primary audience

Solo app founders, indie hackers, and small SaaS teams who can ship a product but do not have the time, confidence, or creative workflow to market it consistently on social media.

### Core promise

Turn one product link into complete, faceless social posts built from hook patterns already earning outsized attention in the product's buyers' niche.

### Positioning statement

For app and SaaS founders who need consistent social content but do not want to research trends, write scripts, design slides, or appear on camera, the product turns a product URL into complete carousel posts and Reels. Unlike generic AI content generators, every post begins with a recent hook pattern that measurably outperformed its source account and is adapted to what the product's buyers actually watch.

### Message hierarchy

1. Outcome: complete social posts for the user's app.
2. Ease: paste one product link; no filming or design work.
3. Evidence: every source hook cleared specific performance thresholds.
4. Relevance: content is generated for the app's buyers, not for other founders.
5. Recurrence: download on demand; daily publishing is available as a clearly labeled private beta.

## Name audit

### Why `Hooklab` underperforms

`Hooklab` names one internal ingredient rather than the customer outcome.

- It sounds like a hook-writing, swipe-file, or copy-testing utility.
- It does not imply complete designed posts, captions, Reels, or publishing.
- `Lab` suggests experimentation and unfinished work, while the paid value is done-for-you output.
- It makes the product appear interchangeable with generic AI hook generators.
- It limits future positioning if the product expands beyond carousels or changes its research inputs.

The name is not unusable, but it forces the headline to explain too much before the visitor understands what they can obtain.

### Naming criteria

The replacement should:

- Speak naturally to builders and founders.
- Suggest visibility or distribution, not just content generation.
- Remain valid across carousels, Reels, and automatic publishing.
- Be easy to say, spell, and remember.
- Leave the evidence-backed hook engine available as the reason to believe, rather than forcing it into the brand name.

### Shortlist

| Candidate | Strategic strength | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| **ShipSeen** | Captures the founder's transformation: the product is shipped, now it needs to be seen. Distinctive, outcome-led, and expandable across every current feature. | Needs a clear category descriptor beside the name on first exposure. |
| **AppToAudience** | Very explicit about moving an app toward its buyers and foregrounds the audience-niche engine. | Longer, less energetic, and sounds more like a consultancy or framework. |
| **ProofToPost** | Expresses the unique workflow from performance evidence to finished content. | Process-led rather than customer-outcome-led; `proof` can also imply testimonials. |
| **ShippedSocial** | Founder-native and immediately signals social marketing after launch. | Less premium-sounding and does not communicate the evidence advantage. |
| **OutlierPost** | Makes the data-backed research mechanism visible. | `Outlier` is jargon for much of the audience and the name overfits the current mining method. |

### Recommended name: `ShipSeen`

`ShipSeen` is the strongest bridge between the audience and the outcome:

> You shipped the product. ShipSeen helps it get seen.

It does not trap the product inside one format or one AI mechanism. It can credibly contain a hook feed, carousel creation, Reel export, and daily publishing while still sounding specifically made for builders.

Recommended descriptor:

> **ShipSeen — social content for apps, built from proven hooks.**

Recommended primary headline:

> **Turn your app into posts people stop for.**

Recommended one-sentence pitch:

> Paste your product link. ShipSeen finds hook patterns already outperforming in your buyers' niche, then builds the slides, caption, and hashtags—ready to download or publish.

This is a preliminary strategic recommendation, not trademark or domain clearance. A current general web search found no obvious active software product using `ShipSeen`, while several tempting alternatives have direct conflicts: PostFoundry is used by multiple social-content products, ScrollSignal is an active social-commerce research firm, ProofPost is an active social-proof product, AppSeen is an existing consumer app, and Seenkit is an existing presentation product. Formal trademark, company-name, domain, and social-handle checks should happen before public launch.

## Conversion audit

### 1. The hero is emotionally appealing but insufficiently concrete

`You built it. This makes it seen.` recognizes the founder's journey, but `this` is vague and `makes it seen` can read as a guaranteed performance claim. The supporting paragraph then introduces carousels, Reels, proven hooks, downloads, Pro, Instagram, and daily publishing at once.

Recommendation: let the headline state the output and desired behavior; let the subhead explain the mechanism.

### 2. The strongest proof is treated as an explanation instead of the reason to believe

The 5x rule is the product's most credible differentiator, but visitors must read deeply to understand the qualification floors, recent-video window, transcript gate, and buyer-niche matching.

Recommendation: translate the evidence into one compact promise near the hero, then explain the method in the proof section without technical jargon.

### 3. The product alternates between `carousel`, `post`, `content`, and `marketing`

`Carousel` describes the format; `post` describes the finished customer deliverable. The customer buys complete posts, not carousel-generation events.

Recommendation: use `post` as the default outcome word. Use `carousel` only when format detail matters and `Reel` only for the video export.

### 4. CTAs describe account state or software actions instead of outcomes

Examples include `Start free`, `Go Pro`, `Read my page`, and `Generate my carousel`.

Recommendation: use first-person, outcome-led CTAs at high-intent moments and plain action labels for supporting controls.

### 5. Auto-posting claims are inconsistent with the feature state

The landing page sells daily Instagram publishing as an included active feature. The account screen says it is in private beta and may not expose a connection button when the provider key is disabled.

Recommendation: lead Pro with stable value—30 complete posts, no watermark, and Reel exports. Label daily Instagram publishing as `private beta` everywhere until it is generally available. Do not make it the primary checkout promise while access can be unavailable.

### 6. Some trust claims are broader than the implementation supports

- `Do I need followers for this to work? No` implies performance certainty.
- `competitors charge $49+ for this` is unsupported within the product.
- `Hooks that already won` does not define what `won` means.

Recommendation: replace these with verifiable product facts: no existing audience is required to create or publish; sources clear 5x plus minimum-quality thresholds; no performance outcome is guaranteed.

### 7. Onboarding explains the system instead of reducing perceived effort

The workflow is genuinely low effort, but labels such as `Tell us about your app—once` and `Read my page` do not fully sell the time saved or what happens next.

Recommendation: make the setup feel like a one-time handoff and show that the extracted profile directly powers every future post.

### 8. Output controls sound technical

`.zip`, `.mp4`, `New visuals`, and `Regenerate post` are accurate but do not consistently describe the result of clicking.

Recommendation: lead with the outcome and keep file formats as secondary reassurance.

## Copy to retain or protect

- Keep the 5x evidence because it is the strongest source of trust.
- Keep the visible source video and view/follower receipts on feed cards.
- Keep `no card required`, but pair it with the complete deliverable.
- Keep `no camera` or `no filming`; it answers a major objection.
- Keep the real output demonstration above the explanatory sections.
- Keep technical file formats where useful, but subordinate them to the outcome.
- Do not claim guaranteed reach, virality, followers, installs, or revenue.

## Phase 3 implementation guardrails

If this proposal is approved, Phase 3 should change visible strings and copy-related metadata only.

Safe copy targets include:

- Visible brand names in HTML and navigation
- Headlines, body text, labels, CTAs, loading states, errors, empty states, and plan copy
- Titles, meta descriptions, Open Graph titles/descriptions, and image alt text
- The customer-visible slide watermark
- Customer-visible downloaded filenames
- Brand references in privacy and terms text
- `README.md` and `PRODUCT.md`, which currently describe obsolete products

Functional identifiers should remain unchanged unless a separate engineering rename is authorized:

- `hooklab.css` and its references
- Package name
- Database tables and migration names
- Internal API routes and environment variables
- Upload-provider usernames
- Idempotency keys such as `hooklab-post-{id}`
- Existing deployment URLs until a new domain is selected

## Approval decisions before Phase 3

1. Approve `ShipSeen`, choose another shortlisted name, or request a second naming round.
2. Approve founder-first positioning for apps and SaaS, with agencies treated as a secondary use case rather than addressed in the hero.
3. Approve private-beta language for Instagram publishing until access is generally available.
4. Approve replacing `carousel` with `post` as the default outcome term while preserving `carousel` where the format itself matters.
